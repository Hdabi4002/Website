TIPS AND PRECAUTIONS

Follow below tips & precautions to safe yourself:


Stay aware of your surroundings.
Avoid using headphones or earbuds in public.
Use well-lit and populated routes.
Be cautious about sharing your personal information.
Carry some safety tools such as pepper spray, small knife.
Keep your phone fully charged and accessible.
Avoid walking or travelling alone at night.

MIND RELAXING EXERCISES

Explore relaxing exercises to
calm your mind in stressful situations.

Deep Breathing

---How to do it:

Step 1- Inhale for 4 seconds.
Step 2- Hold your breath for 4 seconds.
Step 3- Exhale slowly for 4 seconds.
Hold again for 4 seconds.
Repeat: 4-5 rounds.
---Why: Helps activate the parasympathetic nervous system (aka the chill mode).


MORE EXERCISES:

5-4-3-2-1 Grounding

---How to do it:

Step 1- Name 5 things you can see
Step 2- Name 4 things you can touch
Step 3- Name 3 things you can hear
Step 4- Name 2 things you can smell
Step 5- Name 1 thing you can taste
---Why: Anchors you to the present moment.

Journaling

---How to do it:

Step 1- Set a timer for 5-10 minutes.
Step 2- Write whatever comes to mind—no filter, no structure.
---Why: Helps clear mental clutter and reduce overthinking.


Visualization

---How to do it:

Step 1- Close your eyes and imagine a calming place (beach, forest, mountain).
Step 2- Engage your senses: What do you see? Hear? Smell?
Time: 5 minutes.
---Why: The brain responds to imagery almost like real experiences—great for mental escape.

Candle Gazing Meditation

---How to do it:

Step 1- Light a candle and place it at eye level, about 2-3 feet away.
Step 2- Sit comfortably with your spine straight.
Step 3- Gaze softly at the flame without blinking, just observing.
Step 4- When your eyes feel tired, close them and visualize the flame in your mind.
Repeat for 5-10 minutes.
---Why it works: Calms the mind, improves focus, and reduces mental chatter.

~Bonus: It's great for eye health and sleep prep.
